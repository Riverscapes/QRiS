## Overview to CEWA/WATS 6628

This is the main resource page for the [CEWA/WATS 6628](https://lowtechpbr.restoration.usu.edu/workshops/2025/USU/Advanced/WATS-6628/) - *Introduction to Action-Effectiveness Monitoring of Riverscape Restoration Projects* course offered through [Utah State University](https://qanr.usu.edu/wats/restoration/). This course is part of an [advanced series](https://lowtechpbr.restoration.usu.edu/workshops/2025/USU/Advanced/) of courses on Low Tech Process Based Restoration (LTPBR) offered through USU and the [Riverscapes Consortium](https://riverscapes.net). The course is designed to provide hands-on experience with monitoring of riverscapes using [QRiS](https://qris.riverscapes.net). This course compliments the [Introduction to LTPBR Design](https://lowtechpbr.restoration.usu.edu/workshops/2025/USU/WATS-5623/) in the Intro to LTPBR Spring Series and most directly follows on to concepts in the [Advanced LTPBR Planning (CEWA/WATS 6626)](https://lowtechpbr.restoration.usu.edu/workshops/2025/USU/Advanced/WATS-6626) course.
  </div>
</div>


> **Description:** Students learn how to monitor and assess riverscape health through a mix of desktop and field work. They conduct assessments in [Riverscapes Studio](https://riverscapes.net), publish their findings, and present to peers.

:::info
This page appears here on the QRiS documentation site as QRiS users may find it helpful for self-guided learning.
:::

## Prerequisites and Preparing for Class
### Background or Catch-Up Reading

This was assigned in WATS 5623/CEWA 5623, but if you have not taken that course, please read it now. :::warning
If you did not take the [planning course](/tutorials/trainings-workshops/usu-ltpbr-level3/cewa6626), please review the [software installation instructions](/tutorials/trainings-workshops/usu-ltpbr-level3/cewa6626#a-install-and-open-project) and ensure you have QGiS, the QRiS plugin, and the Riverscapes Viewer plugin installed and working before the first day of class. If you need help with this, please show up on Monday morning at 8:45 AM instead of 9:00 AM.
